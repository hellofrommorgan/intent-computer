The Intent Computer gives openclaw a memory that persists and skills that compose.

Every agent starts from zero. It doesn't know what you decided yesterday, what you were debugging before lunch, or why you picked Postgres over SQLite. You re-explain. You repeat yourself. You lose momentum. The Intent Computer plugs into openclaw and gives it two things: a context graph that remembers everything across sessions, and a skill graph of capabilities that grow with your workflow.

Context Graph

Your agent's long-term memory. It captures your decisions, your reasoning, and what you were working on — then uses all of it the next time you start a session.

/context →

Skill Graph

Capabilities your agent can learn and reuse. Not hardcoded tools — composable instructions that chain together and adapt to how you work.

/skills →

The Intent Loop

The decision engine underneath. Every turn, your agent reads your context, checks what matters, and decides what to do — before you ask.

/loop →

How it works

01

Install the plugin

One line in your openclaw config. No new tools to learn. Your workflow stays the same.

02

It remembers as you work

The plugin watches what you decide, what you build, and what you learn. It writes this down automatically — as connected notes on your filesystem, versioned in git. You don't have to do anything.

03

It picks up new skills

Skills are reusable instructions your agent follows — like "extract the key decisions from this conversation" or "find connections to what I worked on last week." They chain together and get better over time.

04

Every session starts warm

When you open a new session, your agent already knows what you were doing, what you care about, and what needs attention. Session 50 knows everything session 1 learned.

Your agent should remember what you meant, not just what you said.
